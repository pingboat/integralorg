The Beginning

The seeds of Integral Foundation were planted during a visit to Sualkuchi, Assam's legendary silk village. What [Founder Name] witnessed there changed everything—master weavers creating exquisite muga silk, yet struggling to earn a living wage.

Middlemen captured most of the value, while the artisans who held centuries of knowledge barely survived. That disconnect between extraordinary skill and economic hardship sparked a question: What if we could build a system where traditional artisans thrive—not despite modernity, but because of it?

"I saw women who could create magic with their hands, yet couldn't afford to send their children to school. That's not a skills problem—that's a systems problem."
