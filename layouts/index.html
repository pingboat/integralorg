Can silk be a solution to poverty, unemployment and climate change in North East India?

↓

For 47-year-old Bina Devi, silk is not just fabric. It's her ancestors' heritage and her children's future.

Bina Devi Photo

You see, for many millennia, people in North East India have been engaged with sericulture. Everything was going well and smooth, until climate change knocked at its doors.

Increasing pollution, pesticides used in tea gardens & beyond, combined with the impacts of climate change brought great dangers to the rearing of silk in the region. It is getting more and more difficult to rear the silk now. And with this, it poses a threat to the livelihoods of thousands of people from indigenous communities.

Our Solution: Integral Farming

It is the fusion of Sri Aurobindo's Integral Yoga and the concept of Vinyasa.

As the name suggests, Integral Yoga takes up everything in its current state to transform it into a catalyst for forward movement. Whereas, the Sanskrit term "Vinyasa" means the deliberate sequencing of activities to create a harmonious practice. Integral Farming is our approach to turn each activity into an enabler for the next one.

What we do

Create ecosystems

Climate-resilient farming systems that regenerate soil and biodiversity. We build complete ecosystems where every element supports the next, creating sustainable environments for sericulture and agricultural practices.

Generate dignified rural livelihoods

Fair income opportunities that preserve heritage and build prosperity. Through Integral Farming, we ensure artisan families earn sustainable incomes while maintaining their traditional crafts and cultural identity.

Boost productivity

Sustainable practices that increase yields while protecting the environment. Our approach combines traditional knowledge with climate-smart techniques to help farmers produce more silk and agricultural products without depleting natural resources.

Connect farmers to markets

Direct market access through Gaongiri and local partnerships. We eliminate exploitative middlemen and ensure 85% of every sale goes directly to artisans, creating transparent and fair trade relationships.

Empower women

Women-led cooperatives driving economic independence and community leadership. Our programs specifically target women artisans, providing training, resources, and organizational support to build thriving women-owned enterprises.

Build local communities

Strengthening social fabric through collective ownership and shared purpose. We create community structures that support long-term collaboration, knowledge sharing, and mutual support among artisan families.

Rainwater harvesting

Drought resilience through traditional water conservation systems. We install rainwater harvesting infrastructure that ensures reliable water supply for silk production even during erratic monsoons and changing weather patterns.

Enrich soil health

Organic practices that restore fertility and sequester carbon. Through composting, natural fertilizers, and crop rotation, we rebuild depleted soils, creating healthier plants and stronger silkworm populations.

Act on climate change

Adaptation and mitigation through regenerative agriculture practices. Our Integral Farming approach helps communities both adapt to climate impacts and contribute to climate solutions through carbon sequestration and ecosystem restoration.
