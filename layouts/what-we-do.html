What we do

Integral Foundation creates dignified rural livelihoods in North East India by preserving silk heritage, building climate resilience, and empowering indigenous communities ‚Äî while honoring nature and traditional wisdom.

Our integrated approach

We don't just address symptoms of poverty ‚Äî we transform entire systems. By integrating sustainable agriculture, craft preservation, climate adaptation, and community empowerment, we create holistic change that touches every dimension of life in silk-farming communities.

Create ecosystems

We restore and protect the natural ecosystems that silkworm farming depends on. By regenerating mulberry forests, protecting water sources, and building biodiversity, we ensure sustainable livelihoods while healing the environment.

  • Native mulberry plantation restoration
  • Biodiversity corridors for indigenous species
  • Watershed management and protection
  • Soil regeneration through organic practices

Generate dignified rural livelihoods

We create economic opportunities that honor tradition and provide stable incomes. Artisan families earn fair wages through sustainable silk production and craft, breaking cycles of poverty while maintaining cultural identity.

  • Fair-trade silk production training
  • Income diversification strategies
  • Cooperative formation and management
  • Financial literacy and savings programs
‚Üí

Boost productivity

For silkworm rearers and artisans, increasing productivity means producing more silk on the same land, creating higher-quality textiles, and reducing waste ‚Äî all while working with nature's rhythms, not against them.

  • Climate-adapted sericulture techniques
  • Quality improvement in silk production
  • Waste reduction and resource efficiency
  • Traditional knowledge integration
‚Üí

Connect farmers to markets

We bridge the gap between rural silk producers and premium markets that value their craft. Through fair-trade networks, design partnerships, and direct market access, farmers receive prices that reflect the true value of their work.

  • Fair-trade market connections
  • Direct-to-consumer sales platforms
  • Design collaboration for modern markets
  • Premium silk brand partnerships
‚Üí

Empower women

Women are the backbone of silk production in North East India. We support female farmers and artisans to develop leadership skills, access resources, and create independent income streams that strengthen entire families.

  • Women's self-help group formation
  • Leadership development training
  • Access to credit and resources
  • Gender-responsive agricultural practices
‚Üí

Build local communities

Strong communities are the foundation of lasting change. We support indigenous communities to preserve cultural traditions, make collective decisions, and build social infrastructure that serves everyone.

  • Community-led planning and decision-making
  • Cultural heritage documentation
  • Youth mentorship programs
  • Traditional knowledge systems preservation
‚Üí

Rainwater harvesting

Water scarcity threatens silk farming during dry seasons. We install community rainwater harvesting systems that capture monsoon rains, ensuring year-round water access for mulberry cultivation and household needs.

  • Community rainwater collection systems
  • Traditional water conservation methods
  • Irrigation efficiency improvements
  • Groundwater recharge structures
‚Üí

Enrich soil health

Healthy soil is the foundation of sustainable sericulture. We teach organic soil management, composting, and natural fertilization that increases yields while restoring soil fertility for future generations.

  • Organic composting techniques
  • Natural pest management
  • Crop rotation and diversification
  • Soil testing and monitoring
‚Üí

Act on climate change

Climate change is the biggest threat to North East India's silk communities. We build adaptive capacity through climate-smart agriculture, disaster preparedness, and indigenous weather forecasting knowledge.

  • Climate-resilient farming practices
  • Indigenous weather forecasting systems
  • Disaster risk reduction
  • Carbon sequestration through agroforestry
